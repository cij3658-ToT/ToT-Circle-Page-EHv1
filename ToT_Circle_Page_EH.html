The Circle: How to apply

Before we go further, a few honest things to say.

You’re reading this because something in the Threshold has been working on you — or because you’re ready to sit with the idea of regenerative leadership more seriously. That feeling is worth paying attention to.

Joining the Circle is a different kind of commitment. Access is neither guaranteed nor automatic. This page is where we explain exactly what that means — and where we ask you, before anything else, to sit with three questions.

How this works

We don’t take payment first and ask questions later. The sequence matters — to us, and we think it will matter to you too.

1.  Sit with the three questions below

In your own time. There is no deadline. When your answers feel honest — not performed — write them down and send them to us.

2.  A twenty-minute conversation with Elly or Chris

Not an interview. A chance for both sides to sense whether this is the right moment and the right fit — for you, and for the cohort.

3.  If we’re both ready, we proceed

Payment comes at the end, once both parties are comfortable. A place in the Circle is confirmed only then.

The three questions

These are not questions to answer quickly. They are questions to live with for a few days before you write anything down. When you do write, we ask that you use your own words — not polished, not positioned, not written with the help of AI. We are looking for you, not a version of you.

Question one

What is the single biggest challenge you’re facing as a leader right now — the one that keeps you up at night?

Question two

Was there a specific moment when you realised your current approach was no longer enough? What happened?

Question three

What would need to be true, twelve weeks from now, for you to say joining the Circle was one of the best decisions you made this year?

For each question, we suggest somewhere between 150 and 300 words. Not less: because brevity here is often avoidance. Not more: because the most useful answers tend to be the ones that stop when they’ve said the thing.

A word about acceptance

We may invite you to join the next cohort. We may suggest a different moment. We may point you back to the Threshold.

This is not a judgement on your capability as a leader. It is a responsibility we take seriously — to you, and to the seven other people who will be in the room.

The Circle works because everyone in it is ready for the same level of honesty and depth. If we feel the timing isn’t right for you, we will say so directly, and we will tell you why.

What we will never do is accept someone into a cohort expecting the other seven to carry them.
